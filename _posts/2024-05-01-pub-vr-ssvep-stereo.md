## Publication Details

**Authors:** H. Liu, Z. Wang, R. Li, X. Zhao, T. Xu, T. Zhou, H. Hu

**Venue:** Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2024

**DOI:** [10.3389/fnins.2024.1367932](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnins.2024.1367932)

## Abstract

We benchmarked binocular SSVEP stimuli inside immersive VR environments, revealing that stereo-dependent target design meaningfully influences information transfer rate, error rate, and participant comfort. The study provides implementation guidelines for VR-BCI paradigms where depth cues and binocular fusion are critical.

## Key Contributions

- **Comprehensive benchmark** of stereo-dependent SSVEP targets in VR
- **Quantitative analysis** of impact on ITR, accuracy, and user comfort
- **Design guidelines** for VR-BCI paradigms with depth cues
- **Insights into binocular fusion** effects on SSVEP performance

## Research Highlights

- Stereo-dependent design significantly affects BCI performance metrics
- Depth cues and binocular fusion are critical factors
- Practical guidelines for immersive VR-BCI implementation
- User comfort considerations for extended BCI sessions
